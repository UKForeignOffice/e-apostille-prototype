Get your document legalised

Use this service if an overseas authority, organisation or individual has asked you to provide a legalised version of a UK public document with an ‘Apostille’ certificate.

{{ govukInsetText({ text: "The Legalisation Office cannot return documents to Belarus, Russia or Ukraine at the moment." }) }}

Before you apply

Find out about getting a document legalised before you apply.

Documents must be suitable for legalisation, including the signature, stamp or seal of a UK public official. If you choose the e-Apostille service option your documents must be electronically signed by a UK notary or solicitor.

Businesses can also use the same-day Premium service.

What you’ll need

To make an application you will need:

  • the documents you want to legalise, prepared for legalisation
  • a debit or credit card to pay legalisation fees
  • the address you want documents returned to, if using the paper-based service (post/courier fees apply)
{{ govukButton({ text: "Start now", href: "/application/1-choose-a-service", isStartButton: true }) }}

You can sign in if you already have an account.

How long it takes

It takes up to 2 full working days to process an application, plus courier or postage time if you use the paper-based service. You can contact the Legalisation Office if your application is urgent.

Contact the Legalisation Office

Contact the Legalisation Office if you have:

  • a question about how the legalisation process works
  • an issue with an application you’ve made

Postal address

The address you need depends on the service you use. You will be given the address when you apply. Email the Legalisation Office if you lose the address.
