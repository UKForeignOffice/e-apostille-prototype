Get your document legalised

Use this service if you need to provide a legalised UK public document to an overseas authority.

{{ govukInsetText({ text: "The Legalisation Office cannot send documents to Belarus, Russia or Ukraine at the moment." }) }}

Before you apply

Your documents must be suitable for legalisation, including the signature, stamp or seal of a UK public official. If you choose the e-Apostille service option your documents must be electronically signed specifically by a UK notary or solicitor.

Find out about legalisation and ‘Apostille’ certificates and the different ways you can apply.

Businesses can also use the same-day Premium service.

What you’ll need

To make an application you will need:

  • the documents you want to legalise
  • a debit or credit card to pay legalisation fees
  • the address you want documents returned to, if using the paper-based service (post/courier fees apply)

You can sign in if you already have an account.

How long it takes

It takes up to 2 working days to process an application, plus courier or postage time if you use the paper-based service. You can contact the Legalisation Office if your application is urgent.
