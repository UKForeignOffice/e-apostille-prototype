e-Apostille eligibility - step 3 of 3

Have the PDFs been notarised and digitally signed by a notary?

To use the e-Apostille service, the documents must be uploaded as PDFs that have been notarised and digitally signed by a notary.

Check how the PDF should be constructed

To be legalised, the PDF must be certified in the UK by a practicing solicitor or notary public.

When the solicitor or notary public certifies the PDF, they must:

  • hold a valid UK practising certificate for the date the PDF is certified
  • sign with a digital signature registered with their name as listed by the relevant regulatory body, for example, The Law Society, Faculty Office
  • sign while in the UK
  • use their personal digital signature, not a company signature
  • include an image of their seal, if applicable, which must be on the same page of the document as their digital signature
  • include their name and company address
  • include the full date of certification, specifying the day, month and year â€“ the certification cannot be post-dated
  • state the action they have taken, for example, certified the document as a copy, witnessed a signature. The certification must be appropriate for the document type, for example, if the document is a scanned copy, it should be certified as a true copy of the original.

If the solicitor or notary public includes a cover sheet in the PDF, it must make specific reference to all the documents within the PDF. Make sure the cover sheet appears first in the PDF.

Information on digitally signing a PDF may be found in the following sites:

  • in England and Wales by the Notary Society - www.thenotariessociety.org.uk
  • in Scotland by the Law Society of Scotland - www.lawscot.org.uk
  • in Northern Ireland by the Law Society of Northern Ireland - www.lawsoc-ni.org
