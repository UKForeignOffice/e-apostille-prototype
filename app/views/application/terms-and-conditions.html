e-Apostille guidance for legal professionals

To use the e-Apostille service, documents must be uploaded within PDF files that have been notarised and digitally signed by a practising notary public or solicitor.

When the notary public or solicitor certifies the PDF, they must:

  • hold a valid UK practising certificate for the date the PDF is certified
  • sign with a digital signature registered with their name as listed by the relevant regulatory body, for example, The Law Society, Faculty Office
  • sign in the UK in the jurisdiction they are registered
  • use their personal digital signature, not a company signature (it must be valid on the day the PDF is certified)
  • include an image of their seal, if applicable, which must be on the same page of the document as their digital signature
  • include their name and company address
  • include the full date of certification, specifying the day, month and year (the certification cannot be post-dated)
  • state the action they have taken, for example, certified the document as a copy or witnessed a signature
  • make sure the certification is appropriate for the document type, for example, if the document is a scanned copy, it should be certified as a true copy of the original.

If the notary public or solicitor includes a cover sheet in the PDF, it must make specific reference to all the documents within the PDF. Make sure the cover sheet appears first in the PDF.
